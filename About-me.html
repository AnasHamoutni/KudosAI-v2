ABOUT ME

My name is Anas HAMOUTNI. I am a Statistical Engineer by training and an Actuary by profession, working full-time as a Senior Actuarial Consultant in Casablanca, Morocco. I hold an engineering degree in Actuarial Science and Quantitative Finance from the National Institute of Statistics and Applied Economics (INSEA) in Rabat.

With over 5 years of professional experience in insurance, reinsurance, and financial services, I specialize in P&C pricing, non-life reserving (IFRS 17, Solvency II), and building actuarial models using Python, R, SAS, and other tools. My day job involves GLM modeling, technical audits, and helping insurers make data-driven decisions.

But beyond the actuarial world, I have a deep passion for artificial intelligence and machine learning. This fascination started during my time at Atlantic Re, where I built a machine learning application for medical scoring of substandard risks in credit life insurance using TensorFlow and deep neural networks. That project sparked something bigger.

Why I Started Kudos AI

Kudos AI began as a personal project born from genuine curiosity. After earning my TensorFlow Developer Certificate and completing the Machine Learning Specialization, I wanted a space to:

  • Learn in public: Writing tutorials forces me to understand concepts deeply. If I can explain it clearly, I truly understand it.
  • Share knowledge: The AI field can feel overwhelming. I write the articles I wish I had when I was starting out—combining mathematical rigor with practical implementation.
  • Build a network: Connecting with other practitioners, researchers, and enthusiasts who share this passion for understanding how intelligent systems work.

This is not my primary job—it is a passion project I develop alongside my consulting work. Every article is written first-hand, every project is something I actually built, and the goal is always to create genuine educational value.

Kudos AI is where I document my AI learning journey in public. The blog hosts detailed tutorials, while the GitHub repositories and Google Colab notebooks let readers reproduce and validate every project I discuss. Transparency and reproducibility are core principles here.

What You'll Find Here

  • Blog articles: In-depth tutorials on machine learning, NLP, computer vision, and AI fundamentals—explained with both mathematical intuition and working code.
  • Open-source projects: Real projects with source code on GitHub and one-click Google Colab launchers so you can run everything yourself.
  • Consulting (on demand): Occasionally, I take on limited consulting engagements where I help teams scope, prototype, and implement AI solutions. This is secondary to the educational mission.

My background in statistics and actuarial science shapes how I approach AI: with attention to assumptions, validation, and real-world applicability. I believe the best way to learn is by doing and sharing.
